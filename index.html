FAQ
Do I need to have the title?
Having the title makes things faster, but it's not always required. We handle title research and surety bond processes for abandoned or title-less vessels — it's part of what we do. Tell us your situation when you call and we'll advise.
What if the boat is sunken or can't be trailered?
That's a specialty of ours. We have the equipment for in-water extractions, grounded vessels, and boats in difficult access locations. It affects the quote but it doesn't affect whether we can help.
Do you work with insurance companies?
Yes. For storm damage claims we coordinate directly with adjusters, provide documentation, and handle the timeline around claim approvals. We've done this enough times that we know what insurers need.
How fast can you actually show up?
Most jobs are scheduled within 3–7 days of accepting the quote. Storm emergencies and marina-pressure situations move faster. We'll tell you a realistic date on the call — not a vague "soon."
What happens to my boat after removal?
Vessels in sellable condition are resold or donated. Non-salvageable boats are properly dismantled and disposed of through licensed facilities. We handle the title surrender and environmental compliance so nothing comes back to you.
Do you also transport boats, not just remove them?
Yes. We're also a registered FMCSA motor carrier (USDOT #4434746) for trailer transport of boats and vessels. See our Transport & Areas We Cover page for details.
Is there anything you won't take?
We handle almost everything. Very large commercial vessels (over 80ft) and boats with active hazmat situations may need a different approach — call us and we'll be straight with you about whether we're the right fit.
